Tale of 3 (Wifi) antennas

Breakdown of different kinds of WiFi antennas
September 29, 2025 · 2 min · 370 words · Me | Suggest Changes

So recently, I got interested in directional antennas after I found this old article on Hackaday A LOT OF WIFI POWER, A YAGI, AND A SNIPER’S SCOPE. For the uninitated, Yagi is kind of antenna that allows for highly directional connections originally intended for TV signals. However, as people found out, this type of antenna also worked with the higher frequency WiFi spectrum.

A traditional WiFi antenna is designed to omnidirectional with the signal extending in a torus-like shape.

Directional antenna’s differ from regular antennas by foucsing EM waves in particular direction direction. The tradeoff in direction is made up by a much stronger signal in the chosen direction. This allows us to catch a WiFi signal over much longer distances ( in case of sniper Yagi a staggering 10 miles).

A basic version of a directional antenna is a Pringles Cantenna as shown in Mr Robot.

The Pringles Cantenna however is no longer focuses the Wifi signals as well due to change in the canister composition (Cost Cutting??). However, it is still possible to build Can-tennas. wikarekare.org has the instructions and measurements.

Besides the cantenna, there are huge variety of antennas which can be found in the wild, including a flat panel antenna allegedly used by Fancy Bear in the 2019 hack on the Anti-Dpoing agency

This got me thinking, how many kinds of WiFi antenna exist in consumer hardware. To this end, I picked up several WiFi routers at Goodwill.

  1. Netgear Nighthawk AC1700 R7000

This is a dipole antenna. The top half of the dipole is above the metal cylinder and the bottom half is the cylinder.

  1. ASUS RT-N12

This antenna I couldn’t figure out. Some sources called it an coiled antenna and other can helical antenna. This looks like a mono-pole antenna with the coil used to add extra active element length.

  1. TP-Link Archer

TP-Link Archer is a dual band wifi adapter, which means that it has two antennas. The picture only shows the top antenna. They have very efficiently made the antenna out of a custom foam-ish PCB which copper layers using inverted-F design. This is also called an Microstrip (Patch) Antennas

Will update after playing around with these ones. Ciao
